How does the system behaves?

Heart
ECG data - electrical activity of the heart.  P,Q,R,S,T

Pacemaker
- Accelerometer sensor - sense motion
- QT-based sensor - Time for venctricle depolarization and repolarization
- QTIs are extracted from the pacemaker electrogram, electrical signal recorded by the electrode.
 
adaptive heart rate- Blending algorithm of accelerometer and QTI sensor.
- Exploits both sensors
- Accelerometer is quick response but low accuracy
- QTI has slow response but high accuracy
	- Physical and mental stress shortens the QTI
- Natural relationship between the QTI and HR so with a regression function it can predict an adequate pacing rate.
	- Syntetic QTIs that reflect the state of the heart model simulation.
	- Synthetic accelerometer signals, either offline recordings or configured to reproduce specific physical activities.
	
Case 1:  High heart rate, but does not do activity and is calm.
	QTI interval are shortened, but no output from accelerometer.
	Blending algorithm will look at the readings and estimate a lower heart rate based on QTI signals.
	It will then send the adequate rate to the pacemaker to the heart.
